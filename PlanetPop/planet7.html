Uranus: The Tilted Ice Giant

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, notable for its extreme axial tilt and status as an ice giant. Discovered in 1781 by William Herschel, it continues to intrigue astronomers with its unique composition, enigmatic rings, and dynamically rich moons. nypost.com +14 science.nasa.gov +14 discovermagazine.com +14 1. Orbital Characteristics & Rotation Orbit: Revolves around the Sun every 84 Earth years (~30,687 days), at an average distance of ~19.2 AU (~2.9 billion km). en.wikipedia.org +3 science.nasa.gov +3 britannica.com +3 Rotation & Tilt: Completes a rotation in ~17 hours (retrograde), and its axis is tilted by 97.8°, effectively rolling on its orbit—causing 42-year long polar seasons. britannica.com 2. Composition & Atmosphere Interior Structure: Composed mainly of icy volatiles (water, ammonia, methane) above a rocky core. Recent models suggest a smoothly varying, mixed-composition interior rather than sharply layered, consistent with gravity and magnetic field data. newspaceeconomy.ca +13 science.nasa.gov +13 arxiv.org +13 Atmosphere: Predominantly hydrogen (~82%) and helium (~15%), with methane (which absorbs red light, creating its blue-green hue) and trace hydrocarbons and hydrogen sulfide. Rings & Moons Rings: Uranus hosts 13 known rings composed largely of dark ice and organics, with narrow structure (20 cm–20 m particles).
